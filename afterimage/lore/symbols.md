# AFTERIMAGE — Recurring Symbols

---

## THE SYMBOL SYSTEM

Symbols serve one function: **give the obsessed viewer something to find.**

A casual viewer watches the visual. An engaged viewer watches the story. An obsessed viewer watches for symbols. The symbol layer is invisible to the first two tiers and obvious (and rewarding) only to the third.

Rule: Never caption a symbol. Never reference a symbol in text overlays. Never explain. Let the archaeology happen.

---

## THE 5 PRIMARY SYMBOLS

### 1. The Cassette Tape
**Appearance:** Held, found on surfaces, visible in background of certain scenes.
**Color:** Black shell, blue label — no text on the label, just a faint circular mark.
**Meaning (internal):** It contains the last record of The Other One's voice. She cannot play it in certain places — only in specific Anchors. The audience won't know this for a long time.
**How it seeds:** First appears in hands, naturally. Later appears in background of scenes set in a different time — establishing it predates the current moment. Later still, appears in someone else's space. The audience should be disturbed by that last one.
**Rewatch trigger:** After the third anchor video, the audience goes back to count cassette appearances.

---

### 2. The 23rd Floor
**Appearance:** Elevator panels. Exterior building shots with windows. One specific window always slightly different — curtain, light, angle.
**Color:** Yellow-orange light source, consistent. Other floors cooler.
**Meaning (internal):** It's the room where Event Zero happened. She returns without going in. She hasn't gone back in. Eventually she does.
**How it seeds:** First appears as a visual detail — the 23rd floor window as she walks past. No emphasis. Later, the elevator buttons in a different video show 23 marked (scratched, worn, circled). Later, she stops at the floor but doesn't exit.
**Rewatch trigger:** "Wait, is that the same building?" — sends audience back to count windows.

---

### 3. The Signal
**Appearance:** 1-3 seconds of visual static (analog grain, slight color shift toward magenta) combined with a micro-audio artifact (the clean audio goes slightly wrong — not dramatic, almost imperceptible). Appears before something true.
**Color:** The static has a faint magenta tint. Consistent.
**Meaning (internal):** Residual time manifesting — the space between what is and what was. She feels it. Sometimes she turns toward it.
**How it seeds:** First few appearances: pure texture, no emphasis. Audience might not notice. Then a comment: "did anyone else see the static at 0:07?" That comment gets 300 likes. The archaeology begins.
**Rewatch trigger:** Every video gets rewatched for signal appearances. It becomes a genre convention the audience maintains themselves.

---

### 4. The Other One
**Appearance:** Negative space in the frame. A shoulder exiting frame. A hand not belonging to the protagonist. A second coffee cup. A dent in a pillow. An empty chair with a coat still on it.
**Color:** No distinct color. Defined by absence, not presence.
**Meaning (internal):** This person existed here. This person may still exist. The audience decides.
**How it seeds:** First appearance is ambiguous — a coat on a chair could be anyone's. Second appearance is the same coat in the same chair but the room is different. Third: the coat is gone but the chair is still positioned toward someone.
**Rewatch trigger:** "Is The Other One the same person in every video?" — generates extended comment threads.

---

### 5. The Handwriting
**Appearance:** Notes, scraps of paper, a window fogged and written on, a mirror, the back of a photo.
**Visual style:** Distinctive — all lowercase, slight leftward lean, specific letterform for "e." Consistent across every appearance.
**Color:** Black ink, dark marker, or whatever medium the surface allows. Always the same hand.
**Meaning (internal):** It's The Other One's handwriting. Every message is either a fragment of one larger message or responses to something never shown. The audience will eventually reconstruct what was written.
**How it seeds:** First appearance: a note she finds and reads. We can't read it. Second appearance: a different note, same handwriting, different location. Audience starts screenshotting. Third appearance: "i found you" in the fog on a window. A month later, another video: the same window, no writing, but she's looking at exactly the same spot.
**Rewatch trigger:** Screenshots, zoomed images posted in comments, collective reconstruction.

| **2:47am** | Phone screens, clocks, timestamps in frame | Event Zero time. Every appearance is this time. |

## SYMBOL DISCIPLINE RULES

1. **Never plant a symbol without knowing what it means internally.** Every symbol has a reason in the lore bible. If you don't know what it means, don't use it.

2. **Spacing:** Each primary symbol appears maximum once per 4 videos. Scarcity creates value.

3. **The first 10 videos:** Seed symbols without emphasis. No slow zoom on the cassette. No music swell when the signal appears. Plant them at natural weight. The audience finds them later.

4. **Comments are part of the symbol layer.** When an audience member posts "is that the same cassette??" — do not reply. Let the question stand. Another viewer will answer with a theory. That exchange is the product.

5. **Never confirm a symbol's meaning.** The moment you confirm, you close the space.
