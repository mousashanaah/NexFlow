# AFTERIMAGE — Lore Bible
## Internal canon. Not for posting. Never revealed directly.

---

## THE WORLD

**The City:** Has no name in any video. The audience will name it eventually — let them.
It exists in permanent late-night. Not "night scenes" — the city operates as though morning does not come, or comes to other places.
Streets are always wet (rain already passed). Neon reflects in everything.
It is a real city photographed and composited into unreality — familiar enough to feel like memory, wrong enough to feel like dream.

**The Time:** Non-linear. Videos are not episodes. They are fragments from different moments in the same life, presented out of sequence. The audience must assemble the timeline themselves. Some videos contradict each other's timeline deliberately.

**The Atmosphere:** The city is post-event. Something happened. Not catastrophic — personal. The city carries it in the way spaces carry grief: the same, but heavier.

---

## THE INTERNAL PHYSICS (Rules That Never Break)

1. **Residual Time:** Certain locations hold emotional residue from events that happened in them. The protagonist can feel this. She lingers in these places. The audience learns to recognize them.

2. **The Signal:** A specific frequency of static — visual and audio — that appears before something true. Not supernatural. More like: the moment before a memory surfaces. When the signal appears in a video, pay attention to the next 3 seconds.

3. **The Other One:** There is another person. They are never fully shown. A shoulder. A hand. The impression of a body in an empty chair. They existed. Whether they still exist is the central question that never gets answered.

4. **The 23rd Floor:** A specific location. The protagonist returns. The building changes — or she changes — each time. What happened on the 23rd floor is the lore engine. Fragments appear across many videos. The full picture requires assembling ~30 posts minimum.

5. **The Handwriting:** A recurring note/message/letter. The handwriting is never identified. It appears in different states across different videos — as if being written, as if old, as if recently found. Same handwriting. Different messages. Or: same message, different fragments.

## THE 6 ANCHORS (Recurring Locations)

These locations appear across many videos. Each has a distinct emotional register. The audience will recognize them.

| Anchor | What It Is | Emotional Register | What It Holds |
|--------|------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **The Overpass** | Elevated walkway, city below | Suspension, between-states | Where decisions happen |
| **The 23rd Floor** | A room in a building | Something lost | The central mystery |
| **The Convenience Store** | Lit against the night | Ordinary made strange | What normal used to feel like |
| **The Car** | Moving, destination unknown | Dissociation, transit | The space between places |
| **The Apartment Window** | Looking out, never in | Witness without participation | What she watches from safety |
| **The Stairwell** | Between floors | Time compression | Where the signal appears |

## THE RESIDUALS (Background Mythology — Never Stated)

The Residuals are people who, through sustained emotional intensity, began experiencing time non-linearly. They appear as background figures in some videos — sitting at the same table they've sat at for years, walking routes they've walked thousands of times. They are the city's texture.

The protagonist may be becoming one.

The Other One may already be one.

This is never stated. It is only implied through visual repetition — the same background figure in videos set across different "times."

## THE CENTRAL QUESTION

**The lore engine is: what happened between them?**

Between the protagonist and The Other One.
Before the videos start.
The evidence is scattered across the universe.
No video answers it directly.
Assembling the answer requires everything.

## WHAT NEVER GETS REVEALED (The Productive Withholdings)

- The protagonist's name
- The city's name  
- The Other One's face
- What happened on the 23rd floor
- Whether the timeline is forward, backward, or neither
- Whose handwriting it is
- Whether The Other One is alive

**Why withhold:** These are not mysteries to solve. They are spaces for the audience to occupy. The moment they're answered, the audience leaves. The withholding is the universe.

## LORE SEEDING vs LORE REVELATION

**Seed:** Drop a symbol, location, or detail. No explanation.
**Reveal:** Weeks later, another video shows the same element in a different context.
**Never:** Explain anything directly.

The audience's job is to find the connection. Finding it creates ownership. Ownership creates evangelism.

Rule: **Seed 10 things before you reveal 1.**
