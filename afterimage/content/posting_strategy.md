# AFTERIMAGE — Posting Strategy

---

## CONTENT TYPES

Five types of content. Each serves a different function in the ecosystem.

---

### TYPE 1: PURE MOMENT
**What it is:** The full 8-part structure. One complete emotional unit. 10-25 seconds.
**Function:** Core content. Builds the feeling of the universe.
**Frequency:** 2x per week
**Lore density:** Low. This is the sensation layer, not the information layer.
**Example:** She stands at the Overpass at night. City below. She holds the railing. She looks down at her hands. She stops. Cut.

---

### TYPE 2: FRAGMENT
**What it is:** Parts 5-8 only. 5-8 seconds. Enters mid-feeling, ends before resolution.
**Function:** The most disorienting, most rewatchable type. Drives "what was that" comments.
**Frequency:** 1x per week (max — use sparingly or it loses potency)
**Lore density:** High. Fragments always contain something — a symbol, an Anchor detail, a moment in the timeline the audience hasn't placed yet.
**Example:** A close-up of hands. The cassette. A cut to the 23rd floor window. Black. 6 seconds total.

---

### TYPE 3: ECHO
**What it is:** A video that directly references a previous video — same location, different state. Same gesture, different context.
**Function:** Reveals that time is non-linear in the universe. Rewards people who've watched everything.
**Frequency:** 1x per 2 weeks
**Lore density:** Highest. Every Echo is a lore event.
**Example:** Same shot as Video #3 — the convenience store at night, her silhouette in the window. But the weather is different. Or the cassette is visible in this one and wasn't before. Or the coat (The Other One's) is gone.

---

### TYPE 4: ANCHOR VISIT
**What it is:** A video set entirely in one Anchor, exploring the space as character.
**Function:** Establishes the location's emotional register. Makes it recognizable for future use.
**Frequency:** 1x per month per Anchor (cycle through them)
**Lore density:** Medium. Drop one small thing per Anchor visit — but the primary work is world-building, not plot.
**Example:** The stairwell. Top to bottom. No protagonist. Just the space. The blue light. The sound of the building. The Signal at the bottom.

---

### TYPE 5: INTERRUPTION
**What it is:** A video that breaks an established pattern in a specific, intentional way.
**Function:** Keeps the obsessed viewer destabilized. Signals that something in the universe has shifted.
**Frequency:** Rare — once per 15-20 posts
**Lore density:** Maximum. An Interruption is a phase transition marker.
**Example:** Everything is the same as always. But the 23rd floor window has no light for the first time. Or the cassette is missing from where it usually is. Or the handwriting says something different from its usual register.

---

## POSTING CADENCE

**Platforms:** TikTok primary. Instagram Reels simultaneous repost.

**Schedule:**
- Tuesday: Pure Moment or Echo
- Friday: Pure Moment or Fragment
- One additional per week: Anchor Visit or Interruption (if ready)

**Total:** 2-3 posts per week. Never more than 3. Scarcity is part of the product.

**Why Tuesday/Friday:** Tuesday has lowest competition + audience available. Friday catches people in transition (end of week, emotional openness, commute/evening). Both days produce strong early-hour engagement that feeds the algorithm into the weekend.

## CAPTION RULES

Captions are part of the world. Use them with precision.

**Do:** Single fragments. Incomplete sentences. Things that could be thoughts or observations.
- "she still checks the window"
- "the light was on"  
- "some places hold things"
- "before, or after" (no question mark — it's not asking)

**Do not:**
- Explain the video
- Hashtag spam (3 hashtags max, all atmospheric: #afterimage #nightcity #shortfilm or similar)
- Refer to yourself as a creator ("new post up!" etc.)
- Respond to comments with lore answers

**The account never speaks in first person.**

---

## COMMENT STRATEGY

The comments section is a space the audience inhabits. You do not manage it.

**If someone asks "what does the cassette mean":** No response. Or: like the comment. Nothing else.
**If someone posts a detailed theory:** No response. Or: a single emoji that could mean anything. The audience will interpret it.
**If someone posts the right theory:** Never confirm.
**If someone posts the wrong theory:** Never correct.

The comment section is the universe's outer ring. The audience builds it. Your only job is to not break it.

**Exception:** If a comment reaches significant engagement and contains a genuinely useful misreading — a theory that points toward the lore in an interesting way the audience believes — let it stand. The wrong theory that feels true is often more powerful than the right one.
