# AFTERIMAGE — Anatomy of One Moment
## How to build a 10-second sequence that generates more engagement than a 5-minute story

---

## THE KERSHAW STRUCTURE (Adapted)

Research identified 8 component parts in Kershaw's highest-engagement moments.
AFTERIMAGE uses the same structure with its own emotional register.

### THE 8 PARTS

**Part 1 — Establishing Texture (0:00–0:02)**
Open on texture, not action. Wet street surface. Rain on glass. The grain of a concrete wall. Fabric moving slightly. The viewer's brain registers: this is a real place. It has weight. Trust is established through materiality before anything narrative begins.

AFTERIMAGE version: extreme close-up of one of the Anchors. The surface of the overpass railing. A reflection in the convenience store window. The texture of the note paper.

---

**Part 2 — Figure in Negative Space (0:02–0:04)**
The protagonist enters or is revealed — but always small, always positioned against a larger environment. City scale vs body scale. This establishes: she is inside something, not the center of it.

AFTERIMAGE version: cut from texture close-up to wider shot. She is already there, in the frame, but we were looking at something else. The reframe reveals her without introducing her.

---

**Part 3 — Movement Initiation (0:04–0:05)**
She does something small. Looks somewhere. Takes a step. Lifts a hand. One second, one motion. This is the narrative event. Everything after is consequence.

AFTERIMAGE version: she picks up the cassette. She stops walking. She turns slightly toward something off-frame. She unfolds the note.

---

**Part 4 — Tempo-Visual Coupling (0:05–0:07)**
The music (if any) syncs to a visual cut or motion at this point. Not on-the-beat pop sync — something more subtle. A cut timed to a breath in the music. A change in texture (from sharp to slightly soft) at the moment of a note sustain. The brain perceives this as meaning even when it isn't literal.

AFTERIMAGE version: the cut to close-up on the object (cassette, note, window) lands on a musical arrival. Not a drop. A quiet arrival — the moment when a tone you've been hearing resolves.

---

**Part 5 — Compression Moment (0:07–0:08)**
The frame compresses emotionally. Close-up. The world disappears. Just: her hands, or the object, or the fragment of her face we can see. Everything outside this is irrelevant for one second. 

This is the visual equivalent of breath-holding. The viewer stops scrolling here. This is the 1-second anchor that drives rewatch.

AFTERIMAGE version: extreme close-up of her hands holding the cassette. Or her eyes from the profile, just catching light. Or the note, slightly out of focus, the handwriting barely readable.

---

**Part 6 — Emotional Peak Image (0:08–0:09)**
Not the emotion itself — the image that contains the emotion. The specific still frame that, isolated, carries everything. This is the frame that gets screenshot. The frame that becomes the thumbnail.

AFTERIMAGE version: the cassette against the rain-lit window. Her silhouette against the 23rd floor. The note with the handwriting visible but unreadable. The wet reflection of the Overpass with her small figure at the center.

---

**Part 7 — Release (0:09–0:10)**
The cut that should resolve — doesn't. Or the action that should complete — pauses. She puts the cassette down. Cut to black. Or: she turns toward what she was looking at. The frame cuts at the exact moment of turn — before we see what she sees.

This is the engine of rewatching. The brain needs resolution. It doesn't get it. It replays to find what it missed. There's nothing to find. The absence is the content.

AFTERIMAGE version: cut to black 0.5 seconds before the natural end point of the motion. Or: hard cut to the next texture (Part 1 of the next loop) before the emotional resolution.

---

**Part 8 — Audio Decay (0:09–0:12 / after visual cut)**
The audio continues slightly past the visual cut, or decays slowly into silence. You see black, but you still hear the ambient sound of the location for 1-2 seconds. Or you still hear a note hold past what the image needed.

This creates a ghost — the scene is over visually, but the body is still in it aurally. It takes a beat to leave.

AFTERIMAGE version: the ambient city audio (rain, distance, electricity) continues for 1-2 seconds after black. Then silence. Then the loop or end.

## PUTTING IT TOGETHER

A single video = 1-3 iterations of this 8-part structure.

**Single-moment video (10-15 seconds):**
Parts 1–8 once. Clean. Minimal. The whole thing is a single emotional unit.

**Double-moment video (20-30 seconds):**
Parts 1–8 once, hard cut at Part 7, then Parts 1–8 again in a different Anchor or time. The second moment comments on the first. They don't explain each other.

**Fragment video (5-8 seconds):**
Parts 5–8 only. Enter at the compression. No context. Maximum disorientation. Used sparingly — this is the deepest cut. Audience arrives mid-feeling.

## THE WRONG VERSION (What Not To Build)

Do NOT:
- Show the full emotion (she cries, she smiles, she reacts visibly)
- Linger past Part 8 (the moment after the moment is not the content)
- Use Part 3 movement that's "cinematic" instead of human — no slow walks, no dramatic turns
- Caption the emotion ("i still think about that night")
- Add music that names the feeling ("sad girl indie playlist")
- Let the shot be static without texture — the world must be alive around her

## THE MUSIC RULE

Music is not decoration. It is the emotional substrate.

**What works:**
- Ambient electronic with space between sounds
- Post-rock without drums (the build without the release)
- Solo piano, slightly imperfect recording (warmth + imperfection = presence)
- Found audio — cassette hiss, rain recorded in a specific location, distant city
- Songs with few or no lyrics (or lyrics in a language the audience doesn't know fluently)

**What doesn't work:**
- Lo-fi hip hop "chill beats" — too familiar, too comfortable, breaks the wrongness
- Pop songs — too much identity, the song takes over
- Songs the audience knows well — known songs create known feelings; AFTERIMAGE needs uncharted ones

**Where to find free:**
- Freesound.org (ambient, field recordings)
- Suno (generate custom — prompt: "ambient, melancholic, city night, no lyrics, slight analog warmth, 90bpm")
- Free Music Archive (cc0 licensed)
- YouTube Audio Library (attribution-required but free)

## SHOOTING GUIDE

You don't need a professional camera. You need:
- Any camera with manual exposure control (even iPhone ProRes)
- A way to shoot at night with available light (neon, streetlights, convenience store glow)
- Patience to wait for rain (or water on surfaces for the texture)
- A performer (friend, yourself, hired) who can stay still and do very small things

All stylization happens in post. The raw footage is just a moment, cleanly captured.
