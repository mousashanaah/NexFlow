# POST-001 — The Place
**Duration:** 10–12 seconds
**What it establishes:** The overpass. Nothing else.
**What it withholds:** Everything else.

---

## THE FRAME

Static shot. Camera does not move. The camera is resting on a surface or tripod.

**Location:** Elevated walkway or overpass, night. City below. Buildings across.
**Time:** 2–4am (the hour when the city is still technically running but has forgotten itself)
**Surface:** Wet. Rain has stopped or is barely falling. Everything has that glazed quality.

---

## WHAT MOVES — WORLD MOTION ONLY

The camera is dead still. The world moves.

**Pick one of these. Not all of them.**

**Option A (most available):** A traffic light on the street below cycling. Green → yellow → red → green. The city is still operating. Nobody is here to obey it. This costs nothing to find in stock footage and reads immediately.

**Option B (most felt):** Rain. Light. Not storm rain — the rain that you notice when you look at a streetlight and see the particles. The sound of it is more important than the image of it.

**Option C (strongest anomaly setup):** Completely still frame. Nothing moves. Except one window — in the building across — is lit. Every other window is dark. That window does not flicker. It just stays on. This is the version where the anomaly does all the work.

**The anomaly must be present in all versions:** One window, in a building visible from the overpass, is lit. Every other window in that building is dark. Do not draw attention to it. Do not frame it centrally. It is in the background. It is for the viewer who is looking.

---

## THE DIALOGUE BOX

Appears at **4 seconds**. Holds until **9 seconds**. Fades before the cut.

**Text:**
```
she stopped coming here.
```

Lowercase. Period at the end.

**Box styling:**
- Dark background, ~55% opacity (semi-transparent, not opaque)
- White text, Space Mono or similar monospace font, ~18–20px
- Bottom-left position (not centered — that's YouTube)
- No border. No animation except a simple fade in / fade out.
- Looks like a visual novel text box. Minimal. Not stylized.

**What this line does:**
- Implies a person without showing one
- Implies the speaker knows this place
- Implies the place has a history
- Does not explain anything
- The viewer's brain fills in everything you didn't say

---

## CAPTION

this place remembers.
```

Nothing else. No hashtags on Post 1. Let it breathe.

## PRODUCTION — CAPCUT (FREE, NO GPU)

### Step 1: Source Footage

**Free stock option (zero cost):**
- Pexels.com → search "urban overpass night" or "city night rain bridge"
- Pixabay.com → same search
- Look for: static shot, wet surfaces, visible city below, at least one building with windows in background
- Download 1080p, vertical if available, horizontal and crop if not
- You need 12 seconds of footage minimum

**Generated option (Kling free tier):**
```
elevated urban walkway at night, wet concrete, city lights below, 
completely static camera, light rain, buildings with dark windows visible, 
one window in background building lit amber, deep teal-black shadows, 
analog film grain, no people, 35mm film look
```

---

### Step 2: CapCut Assembly

1. Import footage, trim to 12 seconds
2. **Color grade — manual adjustments:**
   - Temperature: pull toward blue-cold (left, -20 to -30)
   - Tint: slight green pull (+5 to +10) — this creates the teal rather than pure blue
   - Shadows: crush slightly (brightness -10, contrast +8)
   - Highlights: reduce (+0 to -5, just enough that bright lights aren't blowing out)
   - Saturation: -25 (muted, not dead)
3. **Grain:** Effects → Video Effects → Film → choose any analog/grain overlay → opacity 35–45%
4. **Text box:**
   - Add text at 4s, duration 5s
   - Font: CapCut has "Space Grotesk" or "Courier" variants — use the most mechanical-looking option
   - Color: white, no shadow, no outline
   - Add a background shape (rectangle) behind text, black, opacity 55%, slightly wider than text
   - Position: lower-left, above the safe zone bottom margin
5. **Vignette:** Effects → Vignette → strength 30–40%. This pushes the edges dark without obviously "grading" the shot.
6. **Audio:**
   - City ambient: CapCut has "City Night" in its audio library, or download from Freesound.org
   - Volume: low, -18db equivalent (you hear it when you listen but it doesn't announce itself)
   - No music on Post 1. Silence and ambient only.

---

No watermark (CapCut: Settings → Watermark → off)
```

## THE ANOMALY — WHAT YOU DO NOT SAY

That lit window in the background of Post 1 is not explained.
It is not captioned.
It is not framed.
It is there for whoever looks.

In Post 3 or Post 4, it appears again. Same window. Still lit.
Nobody mentions it in the caption. Nobody points at it.

The viewer who noticed it in Post 1 will verify it in Post 3.
That viewer becomes the first archaeologist.
That viewer's comment — "wait is that the same window" — is the first piece of lore you didn't write.

---

## OBSERVATION FRAMEWORK — WHAT TO READ IN COMMENTS

Post 1 goes up Tuesday. By Thursday you have enough signal.

**Read every comment and tag it:**

| Comment type | Example | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotional** | "damn / this hurts / why does this feel personal / sad" | Emotional layer working |
| **Discovery** | "what window / which city / is the light always on / same place?" | World layer working |
| **Story** | "who is she / she who / who stopped coming / where did she go" | Character pull working |
| **Recognition** | "3am / this is my city / i know this feeling / i've been here" | Place specificity working |
| **Nothing** | silence or algorithm non-delivery | Posting time or visual hook needs adjustment |

**Then:**

- **Emotional dominant** → Post 2 goes deeper in the same place. No character yet. More weight in the dialogue box.
- **Discovery dominant** → Post 2 introduces a second location. The lit window appears again in the background. Still unannounced.
- **Story dominant** → Post 2 moves up the first glimpse. A hand on the railing. Nothing more. Character arrives one post early.
- **Recognition dominant** → Post 2 uses a more specific, less cinematic location. The convenience store. The stairwell. Somewhere more ordinary.
- **Nothing** → Before posting Post 2, change one variable: posting time (try 8pm local vs 11pm), or swap the caption to something with a question.

**The question you are answering before Post 2:**
What did the audience notice? Build toward what they noticed.

---

## WHAT POST 1 MUST NOT BE

- Not a cinematic pan over the city (camera motion — the city already has those)
- Not beautiful in a way that explains itself (beauty that makes you say "wow" closes the loop; beauty that makes you say "what" opens it)
- Not a quote from someone famous, even formatted as a dialogue box
- Not explained in the caption ("this is a place I keep coming back to" — kills the mystery before it forms)
- Not posted with 15 hashtags on the first video (algorithm signal: quantity of hashtags signals desperation on early content)

---

## THE ONLY QUESTION THAT MATTERS AFTER POSTING

Not: "How many views did it get?"

**"What did they say about it?"**

Views measure reach. Comments measure whether the world is working.
10,000 views and zero comments = a pretty video.
200 views and 3 comments asking about the window = a universe beginning.
