# EP-009 — Production Brief
**Anchor:** Apartment Window
**Feeling:** the city as witness to things it can't help you with
**Type:** Anchor Visit (she is present, but as witness — not subject)
**Duration:** 16 seconds
**Cassette:** absent
**Other One:** none

---

## CORE MOMENT
The apartment window. She is at it, back to us, looking out. The city is outside. We are inside with her — but behind her. She is between us and the city. She does not turn around.

## WHAT IS WITHHELD
What she sees. What she's waiting for. Whether she has been here all night.

## EMOTIONAL ARC
0:00 — A window. The city on the other side.
0:16 — She is still there. The city is still there. Neither one will move.

---

## SHOT LIST

**Part 1 — Establishing Texture (0:00–0:02)**
The window glass surface — close-up. The city's reflection in the glass. Cold, blue-tinted, distorted. The glass itself is slightly dirty — tiny marks, the trace of hands. This is a window that has been stood at before.

**Part 2 — Figure in Negative Space (0:02–0:04)**
Pull back to the full scene: she is at the window, from behind, in the lower center of the frame. The window occupies the upper 60% of the frame. The city outside is fully visible beyond her. She is small against both the room and the city.

**Part 3 — Movement Initiation (0:04–0:05)**
One small motion: her hand comes up and rests on the glass. Palm flat against the window. Between her and the city, now: the glass and her hand.

**Part 4 — Tempo-Visual Coupling (0:05–0:07)**
Cut to: her hand on the glass from outside — the reverse angle, looking in. We are now outside, she is inside, her hand is on the glass from our side now. Her face would be visible — but: the reflected city outside covers the glass. We see her hand clearly. Her face is replaced by city reflection. A tone arrives here.

**Part 5 — Compression Moment (0:07–0:08)**
Her palm against the glass, close-up. The city is outside visible through the glass past her hand. Her skin is warm. The glass is cold. The city is cold.

**Part 6 — Emotional Peak Image (0:08–0:09)**
Interior view, full: she at the window, back to us, one hand on glass, the entire city framed in the window behind her. The room is dark and warm. The city is lit and cold. She is the threshold between them. Screenshot frame.

**Part 7 — Release (0:09–0:10)**
Her hand slides down the glass slightly. One small movement. Cut before the hand fully leaves the glass.

**Part 8 — Audio Decay (0:10–0:16)**
Black. Interior ambient — the quiet of the apartment. Distant city through the glass (muffled). 6 seconds. The city is always there, just quieter from inside.

## PRODUCTION NOTES
**Mode:** Filmed preferred. A real apartment window at night with a real city outside is the ideal. If unavailable: AI-generate the exterior city scene and composite behind a filmed window frame.
**Grade:** AFTERIMAGE_MASTER + Apartment Window script. Key: the interior must be warm and dark, the exterior must be cold. The window is the color temperature boundary.
**The reverse angle (Part 4):** The most technically complex shot. Options: (a) Film from outside a ground-floor window with the appropriate setup. (b) AI-generate this specific angle with protagonist_C reference. (c) Use a mirror or double-pane technique to suggest the outside perspective. The shot is powerful — worth the effort.
