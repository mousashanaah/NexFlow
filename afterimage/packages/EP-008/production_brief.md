## CORE MOMENT
She finds a note. We see her find it — from a surface, from a pocket, from somewhere. She reads it. We cannot read it. We can see: the handwriting. She folds it. She puts it back.

## WHAT IS WITHHELD
What it says. Who wrote it. Whether it's new or old.

## EMOTIONAL ARC
0:00 — An object. Paper. Small.
0:14 — She read it. She folded it. It went back to wherever it lives. You want to know what it said.

## SHOT LIST

**Part 1 — Establishing Texture (0:00–0:02)**
Paper texture — close-up. The grain of slightly worn paper. Folded once. Sitting on a surface. Single warm amber light source catches the fold edge.

**Part 2 — Figure in Negative Space (0:02–0:04)**
Pull back: she is in the frame, seated or standing, the note on the surface in front of her. She reaches for it. Her hand is in motion.

**Part 3 — Movement Initiation (0:04–0:05)**
She picks it up. Unfolds it. One motion.

**Part 4 — Tempo-Visual Coupling (0:05–0:07)**
Cut to: the note, in her hands, unfolded — from her perspective looking down. The handwriting is visible. We cannot read it — slightly soft focus, held at an angle, the grain of the paper visible but the text illegible. This is deliberate. A musical arrival here.

**Part 5 — Compression Moment (0:07–0:08)**
Extreme close-up of the handwriting — the specific letterforms, particularly the lowercase "e." Still not readable. But now the handwriting itself is the subject. This is the planting. The audience will screenshot this.

**Part 6 — Emotional Peak Image (0:08–0:09)**
Her hands holding the open note. The paper lit with amber. The handwriting facing up. Her thumbs slightly bent at the edges of the paper — held carefully.

**Part 7 — Release (0:09–0:10)**
She folds the note. One fold. Tucks it — into a pocket, under an object, somewhere it belongs. Cut at the tuck — before we see where she puts it.

**Part 8 — Audio Decay (0:10–0:14)**
Black. Silence — almost. A very faint ambient, interior. The paper settling sound (barely audible). 4 seconds to silence.

## PRODUCTION NOTES
**Mode:** Filmed (real handwriting on real paper — irreplaceable texture)
**The handwriting:** Must be real handwriting. Distinctive. All lowercase. Slight leftward lean. The specific "e" letterform must be consistent across ALL future appearances. Write the note before filming. Write it in the handwriting that will appear in every subsequent use.
**Content of the note:** Write something real, legible in close-up for your records, but staged to be illegible on camera. Suggested text (never visible): "i found you before i knew what finding meant." This is internal — never revealed.
**Music direction:** Single ambient piano tone arriving at 0:05. Warm. Unresolved. Fades at 0:09.
**Grade:** AFTERIMAGE_MASTER. Interior ambient — warmer than the city videos. The note and hands are the warmest thing in the frame.
