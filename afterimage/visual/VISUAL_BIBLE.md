# AFTERIMAGE — Visual Bible
## The aesthetic is the product. Everything else is infrastructure.

---

## THE SYNTHESIS

Six reference points. One throughline.

| Creator | Their Instrument | What We Take |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Kershaw** | The emotional moment — tension held at peak, released into cut | The cut before resolution. The frame that carries everything. |
| **Entergalactic** | The city as painted world — color scripts, step motion, depth layers | Color architecture. The painterly grade. Small figure, large world. |
| **anukitsu** | Memory as image — soft, bleeding light, the dissolve between feeling states | Light that bleeds. The imprecise edge. The glow that means something. |

AFTERIMAGE is what happens when all six filters run on the same footage.

## 01 — WHAT MAKES A FRAME INSTANTLY OURS

**The answer lives in three simultaneous things:**

**1. The Light Ratio**
One practical light source. Everything else falls off. The ratio between the lit area and the shadow is extreme — 3:1 minimum, often 5:1. Not cinematic lighting. Not "well-lit." One thing lit, everything else held in blue-tinted darkness.

This is jabbaaaaaaa_ as foundation. Without it, the frame looks like video. With it, the frame looks like a painting found in someone's apartment.

**2. The Depth Relationship**
The protagonist — or the object — is always positioned so that the foreground blurs and the background blurs, and only the subject holds. But the subject is small in the frame. The sharpness is precise and limited. This creates: the feeling that you are looking through something at something important.

**3. The Color Temperature Split**
The shadow half of every frame is cooler (teal, blue-gray). The lit subject is warmer (amber, orange-white). Never neutral. Never uniform. Every frame is in tension between cool and warm. This is Entergalactic's city-vs-character color logic — the city is cold, the human trace is warm.

**Together:** a frame that looks like nothing else because no one holds all three simultaneously. Most aesthetic content picks one. We hold all three in every shot.

## 02 — WHAT MAKES A SCREENSHOT IDENTIFIABLE

A still frame taken from any AFTERIMAGE video should be identifiable as ours within 1 second. This requires:

**The Grain Signature**
Not digital noise. Not artificial grain overlay. A specific grain profile that reads as analog — slightly irregular, slightly warm in the shadows, slightly cooler in the highlights. The Kodak 5219 emulation in DaVinci's Color Transform library is the closest match. Applied at 35-40% opacity. Heavy enough to read in a screenshot, light enough that motion doesn't feel scratchy.

**The Teal-to-Amber Gradient in the Shadow Layer**
The shadows of AFTERIMAGE are never pure black. They are a specific teal-black (#0a1a1f range). This is the color signature. Pull up any AFTERIMAGE screenshot and the darkest areas will be distinguishably blue-green against neutral black. This is the fingerprint.

**The Bleeding Highlight**
Practical light sources — neon, fluorescent, streetlight — are allowed to clip slightly. Not blown out. Slightly over. The edge of the light source bleeds into the surrounding air. This is anukitsu's glow principle applied to real-world sources. It creates the feeling that the light is doing something emotional, not just illuminating.

**The Silhouette Posture**
The protagonist from behind, hair slightly visible against the light, body occupying the lower-center or lower-left of the frame, city occupying everything above and behind her. This composition is the AFTERIMAGE composition. A screenshot with this composition identifies itself.

## 03 — THE COLOR PALETTE

### THE MASTER PALETTE

```
SHADOW FOUNDATION:    #0a1a1f  — deep teal-black (never pure black)
SHADOW MID:           #1a2f35  — blue-gray shadow
CITY AMBIENT:         #2d4a52  — the sky, the background buildings
NEUTRAL TRANSITION:   #5c6e72  — mid-distance objects
WARM HUMAN TRACE:     #c4854a  — the protagonist's lit side, skin trace
PRACTICAL AMBER:      #e8a856  — neon warmth, convenience store glow  
NEAR-WHITE:           #f0e8d8  — highlights with warmth, never pure white
SIGNAL MAGENTA:       #c44a7a  — The Signal color. Appears nowhere else. Reserved.
```

### THE COLOR LOGIC

**The Split Toning Rule:**
Shadows pull toward teal (#0a1a1f). Highlights pull toward amber (#e8a856). The midtones transition between them. This split is baked into the Power Grade and never removed.

**The Saturation Architecture:**
Overall saturation: 65-75% (desaturated but not drained).
Exception: the practical light source. Neon and warm practicals are allowed full saturation — even slight oversaturation. The city is muted. The light sources are vivid. This creates the painting-in-the-dark effect.

**Per-Anchor Color Scripts:**

| Anchor | Shadow Color | Highlight Color | Dominant Hue |
|--------|-------------|----------------|--------------|
| Overpass | #0a1520 (cold blue-black) | #7a9db5 (cold blue-white) | Cold. Steel. |
| Convenience Store | #0f1a0f (green-black) | #d4e8a0 (fluorescent yellow-green) | Alien green. Familiar. |
| The Car | #1a1a1a (neutral dark) | #c4854a (warm amber, dashboard) | Amber interior against cold outside |
| Apartment Window | #0a1a1f (teal shadow) | #e8c878 (interior warm glow) | The frame between cold outside and warm inside |
| Stairwell | #050d14 (deepest blue) | #4a6878 (blue-gray) | Almost no highlight. Maximum shadow. |
| 23rd Floor | #1a0f05 (dark amber-black) | #e8a856 (warm amber) | The only fully warm anchor. Something alive here. |

**Why these colors create the strongest emotional response:**
The teal-black shadow registers in the limbic system as night/isolation/absence. The amber highlight registers as warmth/presence/the trace of something living. Every frame is therefore simultaneously depicting: the world is dark and cold / something warm exists in it. The entire emotional register of AFTERIMAGE — absence with traces of presence — is baked into the color palette itself. You feel it before you process it.

## 04 — CAMERA LANGUAGE

### THE POSITIONS

**Position 1: Behind (Primary)**
Camera behind the protagonist, slightly elevated, she occupies lower center or lower left. City fills everything else. Lens: 50mm equivalent or slightly longer (85mm feel). This is the AFTERIMAGE default. 60% of all shots.

**Position 2: Close Proximity Object**
Extreme close-up of an object — the cassette, hands, a door handle, a reflection. Lens breathing allowed. The world outside the focus plane is fully dissolved. 25% of shots.

**Position 3: Environmental Wide**
No protagonist. The Anchor alone. The camera is static or barely drifting. The space exists without her. This is the most unsettling shot type — the world that continues when she's absent. 10% of shots.

**Position 4: The Near-Face (Rare)**
Profile, partially obscured, at the limit of legibility. The eye in shadow, the edge of the face catching light. Used maximum once per 8 videos. The rarest shot. The most powerful.

### THE MOVEMENT LANGUAGE

**Stillness is the default.** The AFTERIMAGE camera does not move unless the movement carries meaning.

**The Drift:** A barely perceptible push — not a dolly, a natural breath-hold-and-release of a handheld operator. 3-5mm of movement over 10 seconds. Invisible until you've watched the still version and can compare. The brain perceives it as aliveness.

**The Hold:** The camera locks. The subject moves slightly. The world moves slightly. The frame doesn't. This creates: the sense that you are the fixed point and everything else is in motion. The viewer is the witness.

**No:** Zoom. Pan. Tilt. Crane. Track. Drone. These are content-creator movements. AFTERIMAGE has none of them.

**The Single Permitted Movement:** A slow turn — not by the camera, by the protagonist — that begins in frame and completes outside frame. The camera doesn't follow. She leaves. The frame holds on the space she was in.

## 05 — MOTION LANGUAGE

### FRAME RATE AS EMOTIONAL INSTRUMENT

**24fps:** Standard. Used for establishing shots and environmental wide shots. The city moves at normal time.

**18fps:** The AFTERIMAGE protagonist frame rate. Slightly stepped — each frame holds fractionally longer. This is the Entergalactic step-animation principle applied to real footage via frame interpolation removal. The world is normal time. She is in slightly different time. This is how the city and the protagonist feel like they exist in different registers.

**How to achieve 18fps in post:**
In DaVinci Resolve: interpret footage at 18fps → retime to 24fps. The motion stutters slightly — not like a slow-motion effect, more like film with one frame dropped per second. The effect is subtle but immediate. Something is slightly off about her movement. The brain registers it as significant without naming why.

**12fps:** Reserved for the Compression Moment (Part 5 of the 8-part structure) and the Signal. Maximum jolt. Used sparingly — once per video maximum. More than once and it becomes style instead of meaning.

### THE IMPERFECT MOTION RULE

AI-generated content is too smooth. Filmed content is too smooth after stabilization.

The target motion quality is: **footage that appears to have been stabilized once but not twice.** The camera micro-trembles. It doesn't shake. A single breath-worth of movement, held.

For AI-generated video in Runway: add to every prompt: "slight camera drift, natural motion, not stabilized, handheld texture"

For filmed footage in DaVinci: Stabilize to 50-70% only. Leave the remainder. The imperfection is the authenticity signal.

## 06 — THE IMPERFECTIONS (Required, Not Optional)

These imperfections are not mistakes. They are the difference between "created" and "found."

**1. Lens Breathing**
In close-up shots: the focus shifts very slightly. Not blurring — breathing. As if the lens is alive. This happens naturally with older glass. In DaVinci/AI: simulate with a very subtle 1-2% scale pulse over 10-15 seconds.

**2. Chromatic Aberration at Edges**
A 1-2 pixel split of RGB at the extreme frame edges. Invisible at the center, present at the corners. This is the signature of imperfect glass — real glass, old glass. Add in DaVinci: Effects → Optical Aberrations → lateral CA at 0.3-0.5%.

**3. The Audio Imperfection**
The ambient city layer is not clean. It has: a low-frequency hum (HVAC, electrical — 50-60Hz), very slight variations in level (wind across the mic), one or two sounds that don't belong — a distant voice, a single car door — that appear once and don't repeat. These are not foley. They are evidence. The city is real because it has sounds that serve no narrative function.

**4. The Highlight Flicker**
Practical light sources are not perfectly stable. The convenience store fluorescent flickers once in every 30 seconds of footage — not dramatically, just once, briefly. The neon sign has a 0.1-second dropout somewhere in the video. These are not noticed consciously. They are noticed as: this place is real.

**5. Grain That Moves**
Grain is not a static overlay. Real analog grain is alive — each frame's grain pattern is different. In DaVinci, using the Film Grain tool (not a static texture): the grain shifts frame-to-frame. This reads in motion as: this was captured, not created.

**6. The Slightly Wrong Moment**
Every video contains one shot that is slightly too long — held 12 frames past where instinct would cut it. The viewer doesn't experience this as a mistake. They experience it as: the director was watching something we didn't see. This is the Kershaw technique made into a rule.

## 07 — RECURRING VISUAL MOTIFS

These appear across videos. The audience notices. They don't explain them.

**Motif 1: The Reflection**
Her silhouette in glass — windows, wet streets, mirrors. The reflection is sometimes slightly wrong (the angle implies she's closer or further than she should be). Reflections appear in 40% of all videos.

**Motif 2: The Practical Light Source in Rain**
A neon sign or streetlight with rain falling through the beam. The light makes the rain visible. Without the light, no rain. This is the universe's metaphor for everything — invisible things made visible by the right light. Appears in 30% of videos.

**Motif 3: The Object on a Surface**
The cassette, or a phone, or a cup — placed on a surface (window ledge, car seat, floor). The object is alone in the frame. The space around it is disproportionately large. An object that was held, now left. Appears in 25% of videos.

**Motif 4: The Two Light Sources**
Two separate light sources in one frame — one cold, one warm — and she exists in the space between them. The cold light is the city. The warm light is something smaller — a lamp, a phone screen. She stands in the neutral zone where neither dominates. Appears in 20% of videos.

**Motif 5: The City Through Glass**
Shot from inside a space, through glass at the city outside. The glass catches reflections. The city is available but separate. Available but separate is the emotional center of AFTERIMAGE. Appears in 20% of videos.

**Motif 6: The 2:47 Timestamp**
Once every 8-10 videos, a timestamp is visible in frame — a phone screen, a clock, a car dashboard. It reads 2:47. Never 3:00, never 2:00. Always 2:47. The audience will notice. They will ask why. Do not answer.

## 08 — WHAT NEVER APPEARS

These are not suggestions. These are absolute prohibitions. One violation and the aesthetic trust is broken.

**Faces.** Any face, clearly rendered. Partial, obscured, reflected only.

**Natural daylight.** No sun. No morning. No afternoon. The world of AFTERIMAGE is nocturnal.

**Bright, saturated color.** No red. No yellow. No green — except the specific fluorescent green of the Convenience Store. Nothing vivid that doesn't belong to an Anchor.

**Smooth, stabilized movement.** No gimbal footage. No drone. No slider. No mechanical smoothness.

**Digital sharpness.** Clean, crisp, 4K-detail footage is wrong. The image should feel slightly soft — not blurry, but not clinical. Like something between a memory and a photograph.

**Music with recognizable lyrics in English.** The viewer's brain processes lyrics. Processing lyrics means not feeling the image. Foreign language (Japanese, Portuguese, Arabic) is permitted — the sound texture is present but not cognitively dominant.

**Direct eye contact (protagonist toward camera).** Even if her face is obscured, she does not look directly at the lens. The one exception is the near-face shot — and even then, her gaze moves away within 2 seconds.

**Text overlays explaining or captioning the video.** No subtitles. No thoughts. No quotes. The caption lives below the video. The frame is clean.

**New people.** The only figures are: the protagonist (always) and The Other One (trace only). No crowds. No strangers in focus. Background figures as city texture only — out of focus, in movement, not detailed.

**Clean, modern spaces.** No glass offices. No apartment interiors that look designed. AFTERIMAGE spaces are: worn, functional, years-of-use-visible. The city is old enough to have weight.

## 09 — WHAT MAKES CONTENT FEEL FOUND, NOT CREATED

This is the hardest quality to specify and the most important to achieve.

**Kershaw's secret:** his content feels found because the emotion in it is real before it is framed. He finds moments that were already happening and puts a camera on them. The content does not feel constructed because it wasn't — not fully.

For AFTERIMAGE, this quality comes from:

**The non-posed protagonist.** She is never posed. She is always doing a real thing slowly — walking, standing, holding, looking. Real actions, slow enough to be observed. If it looks like she's performing for camera, retake.

**The city's indifference.** The city does not respond to her. Cars pass. Lights change. Rain falls. None of it reacts to her presence. She is in the city, not the subject of it. This creates: she is real, the city is real, we happened to observe both simultaneously.

**The ambient audio grounding.** If the ambient audio (the city layer) is removed, the video should still feel like a real location. The audio is documentary, not cinematic. It is the sound of the actual place, not a designed soundscape. This is the proof-of-reality layer.

**The unresolved composition.** The frame is not perfectly composed. The protagonist is not exactly on the rule of thirds. She is slightly off. The horizon is slightly tilted. The foreground object slightly too present. Perfect composition reads as created. Slightly-off composition reads as observed.

**The light you didn't design.** Every frame should include at least one light source you did not control — streetlight, a distant window, a car passing. Uncontrolled practical sources are the difference between a film set and a real place.

## 10 — THE 1-SECOND RECOGNITION TEST

How to pass it:

Someone scrolling at full speed sees a frame. They continue scrolling. 3 seconds later they scroll back. This only happens if the frame passed the 1-second test.

The 1-second test components:
1. **The color fingerprint** — teal shadow + amber highlight registers immediately as specific, as belonging
2. **The composition silhouette** — small figure, large world, she is turned away
3. **The grain character** — visible, analog, alive
4. **The light isolation** — one thing lit, everything else dark
5. **The stillness** — the frame is quiet in a way that most content is not

All five must be present simultaneously. If even one is absent, the 1-second test fails.

**The check:** Export a still from the video. Resize to thumbnail size (roughly 150px wide). Does it still read as AFTERIMAGE at that resolution? If yes: the visual identity is strong. If no: the visual identity is living in details that won't survive compression. Fix it at the macro level first.
