## What "winning outright" actually demands (target standard)
To win a 4-5 round pro qualifier outright you are realistically averaging roughly
**4-6 under par per round with no blow-up rounds.** The killer at Q-School is not the
low round — it is the **absence of the 75+.** Variance is the enemy. This is the entire
strategic thesis: *raise the floor, not just the ceiling.*

Your own description — incredible strikes punctuated by 25-handicap misses — IS the
problem statement. A player who eliminates the two-way miss at +2 talent level wins
qualifiers. That is the whole game.

## The Scoring Tree (what must become true, in order)
1. **Eliminate the catastrophic miss.** No double-bogey-or-worse caused by a snap hook/shank.
   This single metric is worth more strokes than any gain elsewhere. PRIORITY ZERO.
2. **Tee-to-green floor.** Fairways hit % and Greens-in-regulation % with a reliable shot
   shape you trust under pressure. Target: a stock shot you can hit 8/10 on command.
3. **Scoring zone (100 yds and in).** Q-School is won on the wedges and the putter.
   Up-and-down % and proximity from 50-125 yds.
4. **Lag putting / 3-putt avoidance.** Same theme as #1 — kill the variance, the big number.
5. **Closing rounds.** Holding state across 4-5 rounds without a mental collapse.

## Metrics we track (logged in daily ledgers)
- Competitive scoring average (real rounds only, not practice).
- Catastrophic-miss count per round (the north-star defect metric — drive to zero).
- Fairways %, GIR %, Up-and-down %, 3-putt count.
- Stock-shot command rate (out of 10) from the practice protocol.
