Caveat: phone stills at varying angles, not hip-height DTL at high frame rate.

## Findings, ranked by confidence

### 1. Club gets BEHIND him at the top — across the line + trail elbow lift (HIGH)
Both DTL swings: trail elbow lifts/flies, clubhead works across the line (points right
of target), then lays off and drops under plane in transition. This is the primary fault.

### 2. Early extension — pelvis thrusts toward ball, spine stands up through impact (MED-HIGH)
DTL driver, transition→impact: hips move toward the ball line, loss of tush line, spine
straightens. CORROBORATES the lower-right-back flare reported earlier. Confirmed predictor.

### 3. Timing-based clubface (MED — needs grip/face-on close-up)
Face is saved by hand timing through impact rather than matched to path. Symptom of 1 & 2.

## The unifying mechanism (why the miss is a random two-way)
Club stuck behind (across-line + dropped under) + lost posture/space →
arms and body in an unwinnable race → two random escape routes from the SAME fault:
  - body stalls + hands flip face shut  → SNAP HOOK
  - body turns on, hosel leads          → SHANK / BLOCK
One root fault, two masks, fired at random regardless of emotional state. This explains
the "happens anytime" report and the 4-5 catastrophic misses per competitive round.

## Fix priority (Rule of One)
**Block 1 — Posture retention (kill early extension).** Chosen first because:
- back-saver (removes the lumbar stress causing the right-side flare),
- unambiguous external feedback (butt-on-stick: in posture or not),
- reduces the need to flip by giving the arms room.
Drill: alignment stick / chair-back behind the trail glute at address; keep trail glute
on it deeper into the downswing; rotate, don't thrust. Scoreboard = strike on tape + does
the glute stay back in video. Exit: 8/10 clean with tush line held.

**Block 2 — Tidy the top.** Trail elbow more in front, stop the across-the-line/lay-off.
Only after Block 1 grades out.
