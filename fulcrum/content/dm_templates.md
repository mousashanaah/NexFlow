## CUSTOMER DISCOVERY GUIDE
### Use in DM conversations or a 20-minute call

**Q1 — SAM.gov workflow**
"Walk me through what your typical week looks like on SAM.gov — how often you're in there and what you're actually doing when you are."

Strong signal: "I'm in there 3-4 times a week... takes a couple hours... I still feel like I'm missing things"
Weak signal: "I check it occasionally" or "my admin handles that"
Follow-up: "What's the part of that workflow that bothers you most?"

---

**Q2 — Time on BD research**
"Roughly how many hours a week do you or your team spend on BD research — SAM.gov, market research, pipeline tracking, all of it?"

Strong signal: 5+ hours, frustration with time-to-useful-output ratio
Weak signal: "Maybe an hour or two"
Follow-up: "Is that mostly your time, or does it pull in others?"

---

**Q3 — Expiring contract tracking**
"Do you have a way to track when contracts in your space are coming up for recompete, before the solicitation actually posts?"

Strong signal: "No — we usually find out when the RFP drops and by then it's too late"
Weak signal: "We get notified through [tool] and it works fine"
Follow-up: "Has that gap cost you a specific bid?"

---

**Q4 — Current tools**
"What tools or subscriptions do you currently pay for on the BD side?"

Strong signal: Names expensive/underused tools (GovWin), or nothing + frustration
Weak signal: "Nothing, SAM.gov is fine"
Follow-up: "What's missing from what you have?"

---

**Q5 — A specific miss**
"Has there been a contract you should have caught but didn't?"

Strong signal: Specific story — agency, dollar amount, reason they missed it
Weak signal: "Not really" or vague answer
Follow-up: "What would you have needed to catch that one?"

---

**Q6 — Decision authority**
"If something like this turned out to be useful, is that the kind of tool budget decision you make yourself or does it need to go through someone else?"

Strong signal: "I can expense that" or "I'm the owner, my call"
Weak signal: "I'd have to run it by my director"
Follow-up: "At $149/month, is that a credit card decision or does it need a business case?"

---

**Q7 — Willingness to pay**
"If the digest consistently saved you 3-4 hours a week on BD research and flagged one recompete a month you wouldn't have caught — what would that be worth to you monthly?"

Strong signal: Names a number at or above $100, or "that's easily worth more than you're charging"
Weak signal: "I don't know" with no attempt to quantify
Follow-up: "That tracks with where we priced it. The founding rate is $149. Does that feel right?"

---

**Q8 — Cancellation trigger**
"If you tried this for 30 days and then cancelled, what would be the reason?"

Strong signal: Performance-based reasons ("if it wasn't saving me time", "if opportunities weren't relevant")
Weak signal: "If it cost too much" before seeing value
Follow-up: "Those are the two things I'd want to make sure are right for you before you commit."
